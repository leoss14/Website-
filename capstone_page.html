Clustering Analysis

Countries grouped by resource production patterns using K-means clustering (k=5) on GDP-normalized production, Economic Complexity Index, and Human Capital Index. Principal Component Analysis reveals two main dimensions: economic development (PC1) and hydrocarbon production intensity (PC2).

Cluster Interpretation

  • Cluster 0 (38 countries): Low-income, resource-scarce economies
  • Cluster 1 (44 countries): Diversified middle-income economies
  • Cluster 2 (13 countries): Oil & gas producing nations
  • Cluster 3 (24 countries): Advanced manufacturing economies
  • Cluster 4 (5 countries): Extreme resource exporters (Qatar, Kuwait, Norway, etc.)

Methodology

This analysis combines production data from global commodity markets with economic indicators to understand how natural resource endowments relate to economic complexity and development outcomes.

Data Sources

  • Production data: Commodity-level extraction (1995-2019)
  • Economic Complexity Index: Atlas of Economic Complexity
  • Human Capital Index: Penn World Table
  • GDP & Population: World Bank WDI

Analytical Approach

  • Resource categorization: Oil, Gas, Coal, Metals
  • GDP normalization: Production as % of GDP
  • PCA dimensionality reduction (2 components)
  • K-means clustering (k=5, silhouette=0.48)

Key Findings

  • Principal components capture 53.1% of variance in resource-economic patterns
  • PC1 (29.4%) represents economic development (driven by ECI and HCI)
  • PC2 (23.7%) represents hydrocarbon production intensity
  • Five distinct country clusters emerge based on resource profiles and development levels
  • Most countries (100+) show low resource intensity, while ~20 are highly resource-dependent
