Reinforcement Learning Framework

Markov Decision Process for order-dispatch optimization

ü§ñ MDP Formulation (Xu et al., 2018)

Ridesharing matching is formulated as a Markov Decision Process where each driver is an agent making sequential decisions to maximize long-term rewards. Unlike myopic closest-driver policies that only minimize pickup distance, the MDP approach optimizes advantage functions considering both immediate and future rewards.

STATE (St): Driver's situation
‚Ä¢ Location: Geographic grid/zone (0-5)
‚Ä¢ Time: Hour of day (0-23), Day of week (0-6)
‚Ä¢ State = (time_index, location_index)
ACTIONS (At): Two types
1. SERVING: Accept order ‚Üí transition to destination zone
2. IDLE: Stay in same location, wait for next opportunity
REWARDS (Rt): Optimization objectives
‚Ä¢ Completion rate (binary: 0/1)
‚Ä¢ Driver's income from completed trips
‚Ä¢ Answer rate (% of accepted orders)

Advantage Function Theory

Mathematical Formulation

$$A(i,j) = r_{i,j} + \gamma^{\Delta t_{i,j}} V(s'_{i,j}) - V(s_i)$$

Where:
‚Ä¢ ri,j: Expected revenue from driver i serving order j
‚Ä¢ V(s'i,j): Value of destination zone (future earning potential)
‚Ä¢ V(si): Value of current zone
‚Ä¢ Œ≥Œît: Discount factor over trip duration (Œ≥ = 0.9)

Immediate Reward

ri,j: Trip fare/revenue

Future Value

+Œ≥ŒîtV(s'): Earning potential at destination

Opportunity Cost

-V(s): Forgone value of staying in current zone

üéØ Key Insight: Natural Driver Flow

The advantage function creates emergent behavior: drivers naturally flow from cold (low-value) zones to hot (high-value) zones WITHOUT explicit routing rules. The -V(si) term gives priority to drivers in underserved areas, while +V(s'j) attracts them to high-demand destinations. This achieves optimal fleet positioning through economic incentives embedded in the optimization.

How MDP Dispatch Uses Zone Classification

üî• HOT Zones (High V(s))

Midtown, Upper East Side

  • ‚ñ∏ Strong future earning potential
  • ‚ñ∏ Orders ending here get +V(s') boost
  • ‚ñ∏ Drivers reluctant to leave (high opportunity cost)
  • ‚ñ∏ System positions more capacity here

‚ùÑÔ∏è COLD Zones (Low V(s))

Newark, Small Outer Areas

  • ‚ñ∏ Limited future opportunities
  • ‚ñ∏ Drivers here get PRIORITY (-V(s) is small)
  • ‚ñ∏ System wants to move drivers OUT
  • ‚ñ∏ Acts as source of available capacity
