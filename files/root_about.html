About

I am a graduate student at the London School of Economics, completing an MPA in Data Science for Public Policy. My work focuses on applied econometrics, machine learning, and spatial analysis, with applications in economic development, public policy evaluation, and market analysis.

Before LSE, I studied economics at Bocconi University in Milan, with exchange semesters at Yale-NUS College in Singapore and the University of Oxford. These experiences shaped my interest in quantitative approaches to policy problems across different institutional contexts.

My current capstone project, in partnership with Moody's Ratings, examines how natural resource endowments interact with institutional quality and human capital to shape economic complexity across 208 countries. Other recent work covers spatial demand analysis of ride-hailing markets, gender-based pricing in UK retail, and regime-switching models of US monetary policy.

Outside of academic work, I served as Senior Editor for Podcasts at The Public Sphere Journal, coordinating episode production and guest interviews on topics in public policy. I also built a political compass tool for the Greek elections that reached over 10,000 users and ranked first on Google for related search terms.
